Overview: Michael Kenna is a British photographer best known for his black-and-white images of unpopulated landscapes and urban scenes. Often employing long exposure times that he further emphasizes in the dark room, Kenna’s work is both picturesque and mysterious. "You can't always see what's otherwise noticeable during the day,” he has explained. “With long exposures, you can photograph what the human eye is incapable of seeing." Throughout his focused practice, Kenna is more inspired by earlier photographers, such as Eugène Atget and Alfred Stieglitz, than by his contemporaries.
